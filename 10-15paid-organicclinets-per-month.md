## 3. The Biggest Untapped Opportunity: Neighborhood Landing Pages

### The Math

Denver has **40+ distinct neighborhoods and suburbs** where homebuyers actively search. Obsidian Denver currently has **zero** neighborhood-specific landing pages.

**Core Neighborhoods (Obsidian Denver's wheelhouse — NW Denver / 80211 area):**

> West Highland, LoHi (Lower Highlands), Sloan's Lake, Highland, Berkeley, Tennyson, Sunnyside, Jefferson Park, Chaffee Park, Regis

**High-Value Denver Neighborhoods:**

> Cherry Creek, Washington Park, Congress Park, Hilltop, Bonnie Brae, Country Club, Observatory Park, Platt Park, Baker, RiNo (River North Art District), Five Points, City Park, Capitol Hill, Uptown, Downtown Denver

**Denver Suburbs (Relocation Targets):**

> Arvada, Lakewood, Wheat Ridge, Golden, Westminster, Broomfield, Thornton, Northglenn, Edgewater, Englewood, Littleton, Centennial, Parker, Castle Rock, Aurora

**Each neighborhood × multiple keyword intents = massive landing page opportunity:**

| Keyword Pattern | Example | Est. Monthly Searches |
|---|---|---|
| "homes for sale in [neighborhood]" | "homes for sale in West Highland Denver" | 50–500 per neighborhood |
| "[neighborhood] real estate agent" | "LoHi real estate agent" | 20–100 per neighborhood |
| "[neighborhood] Denver homes" | "Sloan's Lake Denver homes" | 30–200 per neighborhood |
| "best neighborhoods in Denver for [lifestyle]" | "best neighborhoods in Denver for families" | 500–2,000 |

### Why This Works

- **Low competition for hyperlocal terms** — Zillow and Redfin dominate generic "Denver homes for sale" but lose on terms like "West Highland Victorian homes" or "LoHi townhomes with rooftop deck."
- **Extreme intent** — someone searching "LoHi homes for sale" is actively shopping. Period.
- **Local expertise signal** — neighborhood pages with school districts, walkability scores, architecture styles, and price trends signal expertise that algorithms can't replicate.
- **Scalable** — pages follow a template with unique neighborhood-specific content + same CTAs to `/home-valuation` and `/find-your-local-realtor`.

### Recommended Build Priority

**Phase 1 (Month 1–2): Core 10 Neighborhoods = 10 pages**
1. West Highland
2. LoHi (Lower Highlands)
3. Sloan's Lake
4. Highland
5. Berkeley
6. Downtown Denver
7. Cherry Creek
8. Washington Park
9. RiNo (River North)
10. Arvada (Dax's market — per Daisy Galo review)

**Phase 2 (Month 3–4): Next 15 Neighborhoods = 15 pages**
- Sunnyside, Jefferson Park, Tennyson, Baker, Five Points, City Park, Congress Park, Hilltop, Country Club, Platt Park, Capitol Hill, Uptown, Observatory Park, Bonnie Brae, Regis

**Phase 3 (Month 5–6): Suburbs + Niche Pages = 15 pages**
- Wheat Ridge, Golden, Lakewood, Westminster, Broomfield, Thornton, Edgewater, Englewood, Littleton, Centennial
- Plus niche: "Denver historic homes for sale", "Denver mid-century modern homes", "Denver Craftsman bungalows"

**Total new pages: ~40 neighborhood landing pages**

### Page Template

Each neighborhood page should include:
- **H1:** `Homes for Sale in [Neighborhood], Denver | Obsidian Denver`
- **~800–1,200 words** of unique content covering:
  - Neighborhood overview & character (architecture styles, vibe, walkability)
  - Current market snapshot (median price, days on market, inventory levels)
  - School districts & ratings
  - Local amenities (restaurants, parks, transit)
  - Why Obsidian Denver specializes here
- **Featured listings** (if available) or MLS search link
- **Neighborhood map embed** (Google Maps centered on the neighborhood)
- **Client testimonial** from someone who bought/sold in that area
- **CTA buttons** → `/home-valuation` (sellers) + `/find-your-local-realtor` (buyers)
- **RealEstateAgent schema** with `areaServed` for that specific neighborhood
- **FAQPage schema** with 3–5 common questions about the neighborhood
- **Internal links** to adjacent neighborhoods + `/moving` page + blog posts about that area
